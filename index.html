Blooms from mid-summer to fall (typically July through September).

Annabelle Hydrangea

Blooms from late spring to early summer, often starting in June. Hardiness Zones: Hardy in USDA Zones 3-9.
Tolerates a range of soil types and light conditions, making it ideal for shaded or partially shaded areas where many other plants struggle.
While it does require some attention to pruning, Annabelle is generally a low-maintenance shrub that doesn’t need much attention once established.

Endless Summer Hydrangea

A deciduous shrub with large, round flower clusters that can range from blue to pink, depending on soil pH. The flowers are usually around 6-8 inches in diameter.
It blooms repeatedly from early summer through fall, unlike many hydrangeas that bloom only once.
Easy to grow, but benefits from regular watering, especially in hotter climates. Pruning is best done in early spring, but since it blooms on both old and new wood, it’s more forgiving of pruning mistakes.

About Us

Lucky Shrub was started by a husband and wife team, Jason and Maria, who share a long-time love for plants. Jason is the garden architect. He creates and oversees all designs while managing his team of landscapers. Maria manages all the marketing for the company and oversees the nursery.
Based in Tuscon, Arizona, Lucky Shrub is a medium-sized garden design firm that specializes in garden design and creation, maintenance and landscaping. The company also runs a small plant nursery that sells indoor and outdoor plants, making them a one-stop shop for clients to "transform any space into an oasis you can be proud of".
